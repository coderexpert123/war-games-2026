# Simulation Blind Spots — What We're Missing

## Category 1: ACTORS WE UNDERWEIGHTED

### Pakistan
- Nuclear-armed state bordering Iran
- Baluchistan insurgency straddles Iran-Pakistan border — war could ignite it
- Pakistan's economy already fragile (IMF bailout cycle)
- If Iranian refugees flow into Pakistan, humanitarian + security crisis
- Pakistani military has independent interests that may not align with any side
- **China-Pakistan Economic Corridor** (CPEC) could become alternative trade route if Hormuz stays closed
- Pakistan tested nuclear weapons partially in response to India — any Indian military posture shift triggers Pakistani recalculation

### North Korea
- Iran and DPRK have longstanding missile technology sharing
- Could Kim use US distraction to escalate? Nuclear test? Missile launch?
- US military attention split across THREE theaters (Iran, Ukraine, Korean Peninsula) = maximum overstretch
- DPRK troops reportedly in Russia fighting in Ukraine — interconnected conflict web

### Japan
- 73% of oil via Hormuz, 254 days reserves — buffered but existential if prolonged
- Signed joint statement on Hormuz escort readiness
- Japan's defense transformation (post-2022 strategy revision) accelerated by this crisis
- Yen under pressure from energy import costs — BOJ monetary policy complicated
- Japan-Taiwan chip supply chain deeply intertwined (TSMC Kumamoto fab)

### South Korea
- **Most resource-exposed developed economy**: 90% bromine from Israel, 65% helium from Qatar, 95% ME oil via Hormuz
- Samsung and SK Hynix are the world's dominant memory chipmakers — both under triple threat
- $200B+ wiped off combined market value
- Yet South Korea is barely mentioned in diplomatic coverage — a silent casualty
- K-defense industry (K9 howitzers, FA-50 jets) could benefit from global rearmament

### Africa (Continent)
- Sub-Saharan Africa: most food-vulnerable region, least strategic attention
- Sudan, Somalia, Kenya face compound crisis: food + fertilizer + energy
- African Union has no voice in conflict resolution but absorbs disproportionate impact
- Chinese mining interests across Africa potentially disrupted by global supply chain chaos
- African nations' voting behavior at UN could shift based on who offers food/energy relief

### Latin America (Brazil, Venezuela)
- Brazil: major food producer AND major fertilizer importer from Gulf — both sides of food crisis
- Venezuela: sanctioned oil producer that could benefit from price spike if sanctions eased
- US might need Venezuelan oil to replace Gulf crude — geopolitical reversal
- Argentina lithium triangle — strategic value increases as battery demand persists

## Category 2: DYNAMICS WE UNDERMODELED

### Cyber Escalation
- Iran has demonstrated cyber capability (1,650 vessels GPS-spoofed in one day, wiper attacks on Israel)
- But we haven't modeled: **counter-cyber operations by US/Israel against Iran**
- What if US knocks out Iranian financial systems, power grid, or water infrastructure via cyber?
- What if Iran retaliates with cyber attacks on US critical infrastructure (power grid, financial systems, hospitals)?
- Cyber war has no geographic boundaries — could hit any country connected to the internet
- **Cyber insurance market**: largely untested for state-level attacks. A major cyber event could create insurance cascades rivaling the shipping insurance disruption

### Information Warfare & Domestic Politics Feedback Loops
- Trump pressuring media (FCC license threats, Pentagon photographer bans)
- How does controlled information flow affect domestic support calculations?
- Social media amplification of casualties (both sides) changes political calculus unpredictably
- Iranian diaspora protests (250K in Munich) — could these affect European policy?
- **Deepfakes and AI-generated disinformation** in the context of a war during the AI era — not yet analyzed

### Climate / Weather Events
- What if a hurricane hits Gulf of Mexico during the crisis? US refining capacity already strained
- What if an unusually hot summer 2026 spikes electricity demand in Gulf + Asia simultaneously?
- Monsoon season in South Asia (June-Sept) affects food production independently
- Any extreme weather event compounds the energy/food crisis nonlinearly

### Pandemic / Health Crisis
- MRI machine production halted (helium shortage) — medical imaging capacity degrading
- Desalination plant attacks → waterborne disease in Gulf states
- Refugee displacement (1M+ Lebanese, Iraqi displaced) → public health pressure
- Global health supply chains depend on same shipping routes being disrupted

### Domestic Unrest / Regime Stability (Non-Iran)
- **Bahrain**: Shia majority under Sunni monarchy. 65+ arrested for celebrating Iranian strikes. Deployed foreign anti-riot forces. Could this become an uprising?
- **Iraq**: Political deadlock could break into open civil conflict
- **Egypt**: "Near-emergency" + Suez revenue collapse + food price inflation = Tahrir Square conditions?
- **Pakistan**: Economic fragility + refugee pressure + Baluchistan = stability risk
- **Saudi Arabia**: Vision 2030 disrupted. What if citizens turn against modernization program?

## Category 3: RESOURCE GAPS WE HAVEN'T FILLED

### Water
- Desalination strikes confirmed (Qeshm Island, Bahrain) but we haven't modeled full water crisis scenarios
- 400 plants serve 100M people. Qatar 99% dependent. If attacks escalate on water infrastructure, this becomes THE humanitarian crisis
- Water desalination is energy-intensive — even without strikes, energy prices threaten water production

### Neon Gas (Detailed)
- Post-Ukraine neon recovery: Samsung recycling, but supply fragile
- We have partial data but no comprehensive 2026 supply/demand picture
- If bromine AND neon AND helium all constrain simultaneously, chip crisis is worse than modeled

### Polyethylene (Downstream)
- 85% of ME exports via Hormuz, 50% of global PE capacity offline/constrained
- Prices +50-80%
- This affects: food packaging (food spoilage risk), medical devices, automotive parts
- **Food packaging shortage could compound food crisis** — even available food spoils faster

### Pharmaceutical Supply Chains
- Not researched at all. Gulf region produces/transits pharmaceutical inputs
- India (world's generic drug manufacturer) depends on Gulf petrochemical feedstock
- Helium shortage affects MRI production and medical gas supplies

### Construction Materials
- Gulf states are massive construction material exporters (cement, steel, aluminum)
- Infrastructure projects globally could be delayed
- Aluminum smelting is extremely energy-intensive — prices spike with energy

## Category 4: STRUCTURAL ASSUMPTIONS THAT COULD BE WRONG

### "Rational Actors" Assumption
The simulation assumes all parties optimize for survival. But:
- What if Mojtaba Khamenei (inexperienced, wounded) makes an irrational escalatory move?
- What if Trump's domestic political calculus leads to escalation rather than de-escalation?
- What if a Bahraini uprising triggers Saudi intervention → Iran retaliates → war expands?
- What if a rogue IRGC unit uses a tactical weapon without authorization?

### "No Ground War" Assumption
We assume no US/Israeli ground invasion of Iran. But:
- Netanyahu stated regime change "will require a ground component"
- CIA is arming Kurdish forces — this IS a ground component, just deniable
- If airstrikes fail to produce regime change and domestic pressure intensifies, ground options may be reconsidered

### "No Nuclear Escalation" Assumption
- Iran has 200+ kg of 60% enriched uranium
- If regime survival is genuinely threatened, nuclear breakout becomes rational
- Dirty bomb (conventional explosive + radioactive material) is easier than weapon and still catastrophic
- Israel's Dimona reactor was targeted — what if a strike succeeds?

### "China Stays Neutral" Assumption
- What if the Strait stays closed long enough that China's economy genuinely suffers?
- At what oil price / duration does Beijing decide active intervention (naval escort, breaking blockade) is worth the US confrontation risk?
- The Taiwan parallel: if China sees US overextended, does it accelerate Taiwan timeline?

### "Russia Stays Indirect" Assumption
- What if Russia provides Iran with advanced S-400 systems? Changes air war calculus completely
- What if Russian "volunteers" appear in Iran (as they allegedly did in Syria)?
- What if Russia uses the distraction for a major Ukraine escalation that forces NATO Article 5?

## Category 5: SECOND-ORDER EFFECTS WE HAVEN'T TRACED

### Global Migration Patterns
- 1M+ Lebanese displaced. Iraqi displacement ongoing.
- Gulf diaspora workers (10M Indians, millions of Filipinos, Bangladeshis, Pakistanis) returning home
- Remittance collapse: $125B+ annually from Gulf to South/Southeast Asia
- What happens when 10M migrant workers return to economies that can't absorb them?

### Cryptocurrency / Alternative Finance
- Binance processing $1.7B in sanctioned transactions
- If traditional finance freezes (sanctions, insurance), crypto becomes the payment rail
- Could accelerate de-dollarization beyond what we've modeled
- Iranian rial collapse → domestic crypto adoption surge

### Space / Satellite Vulnerability
- Russia providing satellite intelligence to Iran via Kanopus-V/"Khayyam"
- What if US targets these satellites? First kinetic anti-satellite action in conflict
- Debris could affect civilian satellites — GPS, communications, weather forecasting

### Long-Duration Health Effects
- Depleted uranium munitions? (Not confirmed but historically used)
- Environmental contamination from strikes on industrial facilities
- Oil spill risk from damaged tankers / infrastructure
- Cancer, birth defects in affected populations (multi-decade timeline)

### Insurance Industry Systemic Risk
- Lloyd's and P&I Clubs are the backbone of global trade
- If war risk claims exceed reserves, systemic insurance crisis possible
- Could create cascading defaults in shipping finance
- Reinsurance market (Munich Re, Swiss Re) exposure unknown

## Category 6: TIMING RISKS

### November 2026: Three Converging Events
1. **US midterm elections** — political constraints on war policy shift dramatically
2. **China's gallium/germanium export ban suspension expires** — can reinstate instantly
3. **Northern hemisphere winter** — energy demand peaks, European gas storage critical

This convergence creates maximum leverage for China and maximum vulnerability for the US. If the war is still unresolved by October, these three factors compress simultaneously.

### Agricultural Calendar
- Northern Hemisphere spring planting: NOW (March-May) — **already compromised**
- Southern Hemisphere planting: September-November — could also be affected if fertilizer shortage persists
- Two consecutive bad harvests = genuine global famine risk for vulnerable nations
