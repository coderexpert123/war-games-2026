# Incentive Map — Every Major Actor

## Primary Combatants

### United States
| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Wants** | Quick victory; Hormuz reopened; Iran denuclearized; midterm-safe narrative |
| **Fears** | Quagmire; $200B+ costs; midterm wipeout; munitions depletion → China deterrence gap |
| **Actual** | Militarily dominant but politically trapped. 53% oppose. $2B/day. March 23 pause = off-ramp search |
| **Paradox** | Won military war in hours but can't convert to political resolution |

### Israel
| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Wants** | Regime change; permanent denuclearization; Hezbollah destruction |
| **Fears** | Iran reconstitutes; interceptor depletion; anything short of regime change = domestic failure |
| **Actual** | 93% support. Two fronts. $3B/week losses. Ground invasion of Lebanon begun |
| **Paradox** | Public expects regime change but no mechanism without ground invasion of Iran |

### Iran
| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Wants** | Regime survival; end strikes; Hormuz leverage preserved |
| **Fears** | Internal collapse (Kurds, MEK, ethnic fissures); missile depletion; IRGC/Artesh fracture |
| **Actual** | Succession completed. 92% missile launch decline. But Hormuz + mines + cyber still potent |
| **Paradox** | Surviving but bleeding. Asymmetric tools work but state may not hold together |

## Great Powers

### China
| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Wants** | Broker peace; become Gulf security guarantor; advance yuan; protect energy |
| **Fears** | Prolonged Hormuz closure; forced to pick side; Taiwan lessons favoring US |
| **Actual** | 100+ days reserves. Pipeline alternatives. "Active neutral." Only credible mediator |
| **Paradox** | Best long-term position but suffers economically the longer war continues |

### Russia
| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Wants** | War continues as long as possible; oil revenue; Ukraine leverage; US exhaustion |
| **Fears** | Iran collapses entirely; war spills into Caucasus; forced to choose Iran vs Ukraine needs |
| **Actual** | €513M/day oil revenue. Spring offensive launched. Intelligence to Iran. No direct combat |
| **Paradox** | Winning tactically but multipolar order emerging without Russia at center |

## Regional Actors

### India
- **Wants**: Hormuz reopened. Diaspora safety. US alliance maintained.
- **Fears**: Energy crisis (25 days reserves). $125B remittance disruption.
- **Paradox**: Aligned with side causing its own economic devastation.

### EU
- **Wants**: De-escalation. Energy stability. Strategic autonomy.
- **Fears**: Stagflation. Gas storage at 30%. Second energy crisis in 4 years.
- **Paradox**: "Autonomy" collapses when tested. Absorbs full cost, zero influence.

### Saudi Arabia
- **Wants**: Iran weakened. US commitment. Vision 2030 intact.
- **Fears**: US leaves. Long-term instability.
- **Paradox**: Benefits from Iranian weakness but must coexist with Iran forever.

### Gulf States
- **Wants**: Protection. Economic normalcy.
- **Fears**: Infrastructure destruction. Internal unrest (Bahrain Shia).
- **Paradox**: Need US protection but war is destroying their economies.

### Iraq
- **Most vulnerable**: Attacked by both sides. Oil at 1/3. Budget insolvency within weeks.

### Turkey
- **Caught**: NATO obligations vs regional coexistence. Missiles over airspace. Not invoking Article 4.

### Egypt
- **Drowning**: Suez revenue collapsed. $6B capital flight. "Near-emergency."
